The elements Ba, Zn, and As in a 2:5:6 molar 
ratio were loaded into carbonized silica ampoules inside an argon-filled glovebox, 
evacuated, and flame-sealed. Homogeneity of the elements was accomplished by 
annealing at 900°C for 144 hours. Then, the reaction mixture was ground finely with an 
agate mortar and pestle and resealed in a silica ampoule and annealed at 650°C for 120 
hours. The reaction was allowed to cool naturally, and powder diffraction confirmed 
phase-pure Ba2Zn5As6.
